France 5-for-5 Against Aviators
Fans at Southwest University park got to see not only a home-team victory today, but also one of the most impressive feats of hitting of the season.

The El Paso Chihuahuas outplayed the Las Vegas Aviators en route to a 8-2 win, but the standout was first baseman Ty France. France netted 5 hits in 5 at-bats and finished with a dynamite game line.

"Hey, we tried everything we could think of to get him out," Aviators manager Fran Riordan said in his postgame comments. "We threw everything but the kitchen sink against him and he hit it. Maybe we should've thrown the sink, too."

Ty France hit a two-run home run off Parker Dunshee in the 1st, singled in the 3rd, hit a two-run triple in the 4th, doubled in the 6th and hit an RBI single in the 8th.

France is batting .355 with 1 home runs and 7 RBIs this season.
