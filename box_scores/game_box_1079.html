Moustakas Swings for the Cycle
It is rare for players to collect a single, double, triple and home run in one game, so it's a special moment when it happens.

Mike Moustakas savored such a moment today, going 4-for-5 and hitting for the cycle against the Washington Nationals. Unfortunately for Moustakas, that was all he could savor, as his Cincinnati Reds lost the contest by a score of 10-9.

After the game, the third baseman was in no mood to talk about the hitting feat, calling it "pointless trivia."

"Nobody cares if I went 0-for-5 or 1-for-5 or 4-for-5," Moustakas said. "10-9. That's the only line anyone around here cares about today."

Mike Moustakas singled in the 1st, struck out in the 3rd, hit a solo-shot off Roenis Elias in the 5th, doubled in the 7th and hit an RBI triple in the 8th.

Moustakas is hitting .281 this year with 11 home runs and 27 RBIs. He also has an OBP of .336, 9 walks and scored 20 times.
