Stevenson Belts 5 Hits
After a 5-hit effort to lead Fresno's 13-7 victory over the Las Vegas Aviators, left fielder Andrew Stevenson told BNN beat writers it had been "a special day."

"It really doesn't matter whether it's sandlot ball, high school or college, two hits in a game is nice," he said. "Three or four is better, but five or more is out of sight. And to do that at the Pacific Coast League-level is really something special."

The 25-year-old player earned his accolades today at Las Vegas Ballpark with a spectacular 5-for-6 game against the Aviators.

"His swing is getting there," said Grizzlies skipper Randy Knorr, who spoke glowingly of Stevenson after the game. "We want him to be quick with his swing, short and compact. We want him to be patient and go the other way when he can. Andrew did that to perfection today."

Andrew Stevenson doubled in the 1st, singled in the 2nd, singled in the 4th, hit a GRAND SLAM off Parker Dunshee in the 5th, flied out in the 7th and singled in the 9th.

For the season, Stevenson is hitting at a .414 clip with 2 home runs, 10 RBIs and has scored 16 runs. He has 29 hits, 6 walks and a .461 on-base percentage.
