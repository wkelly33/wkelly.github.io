Stallings scores 3 runs as Pirates Squeeze Past Miami
Pittsburgh catcher Jacob Stallings made his presence felt in the 8-7 Pirates triumph over the Miami Marlins. Stallings was 2 for 4 with a home run, while scoring 3 times and driving in 4. Pittsburgh starter Joe Musgrove was credited with the win after throwing 96 pitches over 6.2 innings. His record now stands at 2-3. Keone Kela closed it out for his 3rd save.

Pittsburgh took a 6-2 lead on Stallings's 1st home run of the season. The grand slam home run, which he hit off Sandy Alcantara in the top of the sixth, propelled Pittsburgh to the win.

"It feels good to get this one," Stallings told the Pittsburgh Herald.

Miami falls to 11-21.
